## Design Principles for MBKRU

### 1. **Clarity Over Clutter**
- One main idea in the hero: the direct voice between President and people.
- Short supporting line, not long paragraphs.
- Premium civic sites use restraint and clear hierarchy.

### 2. **Typography as the Lead**
- Large, confident headlines.
- Type pairing: serif for authority (e.g. Playfair), sans for clarity.
- Typography as identity, not decoration.

### 3. **Impact Metrics Up Front**
- Lead with numbers: 5 pillars, 16 regions, 100% non-partisan.
- Transparency International: “250,000+ people helped.”
- BudgIT: “22m citizens reached.”
- MBKRU: surface stats early and clearly.

### 4. **Action-Oriented CTAs**
- Common Cause: “Take Action,” “Donate,” “Join.”
- VoteRiders: “Donate,” “Volunteer,” “Subscribe.”
- One primary CTA, one secondary; both visible and clear.

### 5. **Full Bleed, Immersive Hero**
- Full-width hero, not a boxed section.
- Image + overlay that feels like an environment, not a banner.
- Editorial feel: strong type + image in a clear sequence.

### 6. **Quiet Minimalism**
- Few elements, each justified.
- Generous spacing.
- No decorative clutter.

### 7. **Subtle Motion**
- Soft fades, light transitions.
- Motion that guides the eye, not distracts.
- Premium feel through small, purposeful animation.

### 8. **Color & Trust**
- Advocacy Through Walls: warm cream + dark.
- Transparency International: clean, institutional.
- MBKRU: teal + gold + warm accent; keep palette tight and intentional.

## Concrete Recommendations for MBKRU

### Hero Section
- **Headline:** One strong line. “A Direct Voice Between the President and the People of Ghana” is strong; consider tightening to a single, memorable phrase.
- **Subhead:** One line only (e.g. “Advocate • Watchdog • Catalyst”).
- **Layout:** Editorial—headline left, image or visual right; or full bleed with centered type.
- **Motion:** Subtle fade-in, staggered text.
- **CTA:** One primary (“Get in Touch”), one secondary (“Learn More”).

### Top Bar + Header + Hero
- Treat as one dark “canopy”: same teal, no hard edges.
- Gold accent line as a continuous thread.
- Header: light glass effect (backdrop-blur) so it feels part of the hero.

### Pillar Cards
- Less frosted glass, more editorial.
- Consider: icon + title + one-line description.
- Hover: subtle lift or gold accent, not heavy effects.

### Stats / Trust Section
- Move stats higher (e.g. under hero or in hero).
- Large numbers, short labels.
- “5 Pillars • 16 Regions • 100% Non-Partisan • SDG 1”

### Typography
- Headlines: larger, bolder.
- More contrast between H1 and body.
- Consider a stronger display font for key headlines.

### Photography
- Use imagery that feels editorial and authentic.
- Ghana-specific where possible.
- Avoid generic stock; prefer real community, engagement, or civic moments.

## 2025 Trends to Adopt

1. **Bold typography** — Oversized headlines, clear hierarchy.
2. **Color blocking** — Strong teal blocks, not only gradients.
3. **Asymmetry** — Break the grid where it adds interest.
4. **Data visualization** — Where relevant (e.g. regions, impact).
5. **Editorial layouts** — Magazine-style sections, not generic blocks.

---

## Quick Wins (Implement First)

1. **Hero:** Sharper headline hierarchy, more whitespace, one clear CTA.
2. **Stats:** Add a compact stats row in or just below the hero.
3. **Top bar → hero:** Ensure seamless gradient and gold accent.
4. **Pillar cards:** Simplify to icon + title + one line; lighter styling.
5. **Typography:** Increase headline size and weight.
